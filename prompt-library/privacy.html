Privacy

Last updated: 2026-04-23

prompt-library is a small experiment. This page describes what data we collect, why, and how long we keep it. If anything here is unclear, open an issue on the repo.

What we store

  • Email address — if you sign in with a magic link. Held by our auth provider (Supabase).
  • GitHub handle and avatar URL — if you sign in with GitHub.
  • Profile fields you enter — handle, bio, optional X handle.
  • Prompts you submit — text, metadata, and a link back to your profile.
  • Prompts you like — which prompt, which user or anonymous fingerprint.
  • Anonymous like fingerprint — a hash of your browser user agent + language + screen size, used only to prevent duplicate anonymous likes. Not cross-site trackable.

What we do NOT do

  • No analytics trackers, ad pixels, or third-party embeds.
  • No selling or sharing of data with third parties.
  • No server-side logs of your browsing beyond Supabase's operational logs.

Who has access

Only the project maintainer. Your email is never shown to other users. Your profile (handle, bio, avatar, submitted prompts) is public by design.

Deleting your account

Email the maintainer and your account plus all associated submissions and likes will be removed within a reasonable timeframe. A self-service delete button is on the roadmap.

Cookies

A single auth cookie from Supabase keeps you signed in. No analytics or advertising cookies.

Changes

If this policy changes materially, the "Last updated" date above will change. For a site this small there is no mailing list; check back here.

Contact

Open an issue on the GitHub repo.
